<p><p>Eosinophilic esophagitis is a chronic immune-mediated disorder characterized by inflammation of the esophagus caused by an overabundance of eosinophils. The treatment of eosinophilic esophagitis aims to reduce inflammation, alleviate symptoms, and prevent long-term complications. It typically involves a combination of dietary changes, medication, and in some cases, dilation of the esophagus.</p><p>Dietary therapy is an essential component of eosinophilic esophagitis treatment. Elimination diets, such as the six-food elimination diet (SFED) or elemental diet, involve removing specific foods or all foods except essential nutrients from the patient's diet. This approach helps identify trigger foods causing inflammation and facilitates the management of symptoms.</p><p>Medication options for eosinophilic esophagitis include proton pump inhibitors (PPIs), corticosteroids (topical or systemic), and targeted therapies like biologic agents. PPIs help reduce acid levels in the stomach and may alleviate symptoms in some patients. Corticosteroids can be administered orally or topically through swallowed aerosols; they help reduce inflammation. Biologic agents, such as monoclonal antibodies, are relatively new treatment options that target specific molecules involved in the inflammatory response.</p><p>In terms of the market growth analysis and latest trends, the eosinophilic esophagitis treatment market is expected to witness strong growth during the forecast period.

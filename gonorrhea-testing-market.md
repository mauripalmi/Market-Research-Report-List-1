<p><p>Gonorrhea testing methods vary and include Nucleic Acid Amplification (NAA), Gram Stain, Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA), Gonorrhea Culture, and Rapid test market. NAA is highly sensitive, detecting Gonorrhea DNA for accurate results. Gram Stain is a classical method where a sample is examined under a microscope for specific bacterial characteristics. ELISA detects Gonorrhea antibodies in blood or urine samples. Gonorrhea Culture involves growing bacteria in a lab to identify the infection. Rapid tests provide quick results, relying on antigen or antibody detection, but may have lower sensitivity compared to other methods.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Noninvasive Hemodynamic Monitoring Devices</li><li>Minimally Invasive Hemodynamic Monitoring Devices</li><li>Invasive Hemodynamic Monitoring Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hemodynamic monitoring devices are used to measure the flow and pressure of blood in a patient's body. Noninvasive devices are external sensors that measure blood flow and pressure without any invasive procedures. Minimally invasive devices involve inserting small catheters into blood vessels or heart chambers to directly monitor blood flow and pressure. Invasive devices require surgically implanting a catheter into a major blood vessel or heart chamber. These devices provide more accurate and detailed measurements, but carry higher risks and require advanced medical expertise.</p></p>

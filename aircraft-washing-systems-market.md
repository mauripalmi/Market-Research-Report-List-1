<p><p>The aircraft washing systems market is divided into two main types: dry and wet systems. Dry aircraft washing systems involve using special cleaning agents, such as dry wash solutions or powders, along with microfiber cloths or brushes to remove dirt and grime from the aircraft surface without the need for water. On the other hand, wet aircraft washing systems rely on the traditional method of washing with water, combined with cleaning agents, brushes, and sometimes pressure washers, to thoroughly clean the aircraft. Both systems have their distinct advantages and are used based on various factors such as time, cost, and environmental considerations.</p></p>

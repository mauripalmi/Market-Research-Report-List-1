<p><p>Shunt reactors are used in power systems to compensate for reactive power and improve voltage stability. The market for these reactors is divided into two types: </p><p>1. Air-core Dry Type: These reactors do not contain any iron core and use air as the medium for heat dissipation. They are lightweight, compact, and require minimal maintenance. However, they have lower power ratings and are less efficient compared to oil-immersed reactors. </p><p>2. Oil Immersed Type: These reactors use oil as an insulation and cooling medium. They have higher power ratings and better efficiency compared to air-core dry type reactors. However, they are larger in size, require regular oil monitoring, and maintenance.</p></p>

<p><p>Textural food ingredients refer to substances used in food products to enhance or modify their texture. These ingredients can be divided into three main types: thickeners, emulsifiers, and stabilizers. Thickeners add viscosity and texture to food, improving mouthfeel and preventing separation. Emulsifiers help mix ingredients that would typically separate, such as oil and water, creating a more uniform texture. Stabilizers prevent undesired changes in texture by maintaining the structure and consistency of food products. These textural food ingredients play a crucial role in enhancing the overall sensory experience of various food items.</p></p>

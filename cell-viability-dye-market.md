<p><p>Cell viability dye is a type of fluorescent dye used in cell biology and cytotoxicity studies to determine the viability and health of cells. These dyes are designed to enter live cells with intact plasma membranes and stain the nuclei or cytoplasm to enable scientists to differentiate between live and dead cells. The dye emits a fluorescence signal that can be detected using fluorescence microscopy or flow cytometry.</p><p>The cell viability dye market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing applications of cell viability assays in drug discovery, stem cell research, cancer biology, and immunology. The ability of these dyes to provide quick and accurate results on cell viability has made them an essential tool in various research and clinical applications. Moreover, advancements in dye technology, such as the development of high-throughput screening assays and the introduction of multiplexing systems, have further propelled the market growth.</p><p>Additionally, the rising prevalence of chronic diseases and the increasing demand for personalized medicine are driving the demand for cell viability dyes. <p><ul><li>DNA Binding Dye</li><li>Amine Reactive Dye</li><li>Enzyme Activated Dye</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Cell Viability Dye Market encompasses various types of dyes used to assess cell viability. DNA Binding Dyes bind to nucleic acids in live cells, indicating their viability. Amine Reactive Dyes are permeable to live cells and react with primary amines, providing information on cell viability. Enzyme Activated Dyes are designed to target specific enzymes active in living cells and exhibit fluorescence when metabolized, representing cell viability. These dyes play a crucial role in research and diagnostics by enabling accurate and efficient assessment of cell viability in different contexts.</p></p>

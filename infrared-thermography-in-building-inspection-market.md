<p><p>Infrared thermography in building inspection is a non-destructive testing method used to identify anomalies or defects in buildings by detecting thermal patterns. This technology can help identify issues such as water leaks, insulation defects, electrical faults, and energy efficiency problems.</p><p>The infrared thermography in building inspection market has experienced significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue to expand at a CAGR of 5% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, there is an increasing focus on energy conservation and sustainability, which has led to an increased demand for building inspections to identify energy efficiency issues. Infrared thermography is a valuable tool in this regard as it can identify areas of heat loss and energy wastage.</p><p>Moreover, the growing awareness of the importance of preventive maintenance in buildings has also contributed to the market growth. Building owners and managers are realizing the cost-saving benefits of identifying and resolving issues before they become major problems, and infrared thermography enables early detection of potential faults.</p><p>In terms of the latest trends, there is a growing adoption of advanced infrared technology in building inspections. Manufacturers are developing cameras with higher resolution and sensitivity, enabling more accurate and detailed analysis of thermal patterns. Additionally, there is an increasing integration of infrared thermography with other building inspection techniques, such as moisture meters and structural analysis tools, to provide a more comprehensive assessment of building conditions.</p><p>Overall, the infrared thermography in building inspection market is expected to witness steady growth due to the increasing demand for energy-efficient buildings and the importance of preventive maintenance. Technological advancements and the integration of infrared thermography with other inspection techniques will further drive the market in the coming years.</p></p>

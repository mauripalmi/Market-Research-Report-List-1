<p><p>The Cardiovascular Disease Diagnostic Equipment Market is divided into two main types: In Vitro Diagnostics and In Vivo Diagnostics. In Vitro Diagnostics refers to tests and procedures performed outside the body, typically on blood or tissue samples in a laboratory setting. This includes blood tests, cholesterol tests, and genetic testing. In Vivo Diagnostics, on the other hand, involves tests and procedures conducted inside the body, directly on the cardiovascular system. This includes imaging techniques like echocardiograms, angiography, and cardiac catheterization. Both types of diagnostics play a crucial role in diagnosing and monitoring cardiovascular diseases.</p></p>

<p><p>Medical hydrophilic coatings are specially designed coatings that enhance the lubricity and hydrophilicity of medical devices, such as catheters, guidewires, and syringes, to improve their performance during medical procedures. Nano coating involves applying ultra-thin layers of hydrophilic materials at the nanoscale. Metal coating utilizes metals like gold or silver to achieve the desired hydrophilicity. Polymer coating uses hydrophilic polymers, such as polyethylene glycol (PEG), to create a lubricious surface. Other types of hydrophilic coatings include antimicrobial coatings and drug-eluting coatings, which release therapeutic agents to prevent infections or treat specific conditions.</p></p>

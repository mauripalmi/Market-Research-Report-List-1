<p><p>Laboratory refractometers are devices used to measure the refractive index of substances. There are two main types of laboratory refractometers: benchtop and portable. </p><p>Benchtop refractometers are larger and designed for use in a fixed location, such as a laboratory. They offer high precision, accuracy, and have advanced features like digital displays and temperature-controlled chambers. These refractometers are ideal for applications that require precise measurements and where portability is not essential. </p><p>On the other hand, portable refractometers are smaller and lightweight, making them suitable for on-site analysis or fieldwork. They are compact, easy to carry, and offer quick and convenient measurements. Portable refractometers are commonly used in industries such as agriculture, food and beverages, and automotive for quality control and process monitoring purposes.</p></p>

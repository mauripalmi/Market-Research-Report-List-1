<p><p>Dermal substitutes are alternatives used in skin grafting procedures when there is a need to replace damaged or lost skin. There are three types of dermal substitutes: synthetic, biosynthetic, and biological. </p><p>Synthetic dermal substitutes are made of man-made materials, such as silicon or collagen-based polymers. Biosynthetic substitutes combine synthetic materials with a small amount of biological components, often collagen from animals. Biological substitutes consist entirely of living human or animal cells or tissues, often derived from donors or obtained through tissue engineering techniques. Each type offers different benefits and considerations when used in skin grafting procedures.</p></p>

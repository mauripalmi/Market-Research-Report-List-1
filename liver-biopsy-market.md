<p><p>Liver biopsy is a medical procedure used to collect liver tissue samples for diagnostic purposes. There are three main types of liver biopsy procedures. Percutaneous liver biopsy involves inserting a needle through the skin to reach the liver. Transjugular liver biopsy uses a catheter to access the liver through a vein in the neck. Laparoscopic liver biopsy involves making small incisions in the abdomen to access and collect liver tissue. These different market types allow doctors to choose the most suitable method based on the patient's condition and specific needs.</p></p>

<p><p>Latent Tuberculosis Infection (LTBI) Testing is a diagnostic procedure used to identify individuals who have been infected with Mycobacterium tuberculosis, the bacteria that causes tuberculosis (TB), but do not have active symptoms of the disease. LTBI testing is crucial for the prevention and control of TB transmission, as it allows healthcare providers to identify and treat individuals who are at risk of eventually developing active TB. </p><p>There are two main types of LTBI tests: tuberculin skin tests (TST) and interferon-gamma release assays (IGRAs). TST involves injecting a small amount of fluid called tuberculin into the skin of the forearm and observing for a specific reaction. IGRAs, on the other hand, measure the release of interferon-gamma by specific immune cells when exposed to TB proteins. Both tests have their advantages and limitations, and the choice of test may vary depending on the individual's characteristics and resource availability.</p><p>The global Latent Tuberculosis Infection (LTBI) Testing Market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. Factors driving market growth include the increasing prevalence of TB infections, rising awareness about the importance of LTBI testing, and the implementation of government initiatives and programs to prevent TB transmission. Additionally, advancements in diagnostic technologies and the development of novel LTBI testing methods are expected to fuel market growth.</p><p>Emerging trends in the LTBI testing market include the integration of molecular techniques in LTBI diagnostics, the introduction of point-of-care testing devices for rapid and accurate results, and the focus on developing cost-effective and efficient testing methods. Furthermore, the COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the need for comprehensive infectious disease testing, including LTBI testing, leading to increased market demand.</p><p>In conclusion, the Latent Tuberculosis Infection (LTBI) Testing Market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period due to increasing awareness, government initiatives, technological advancements, and a rising prevalence of TB infections. <p><p>The Latent Tuberculosis Infection (LTBI) Testing market includes two main types of diagnostic tests: the Tuberculin Skin Test (TST) and the Interferon Gamma Released Assay (IGRA).

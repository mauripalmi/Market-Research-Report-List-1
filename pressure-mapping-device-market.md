<p><p>Pressure mapping devices are used to assess the pressure distribution on a person's body. In the market, there are three main types: </p><p>1) Wearable products: These devices can be attached to the body, such as insoles or shoe inserts, to measure pressure while walking or running. They provide real-time feedback and can be used for sports performance or injury prevention.</p><p>2) Body supporting surfaces: These devices are used on surfaces like mattresses, chairs, or cushions to measure pressure distribution when a person is sitting or lying down. They are commonly used in healthcare settings to prevent pressure ulcers or improve comfort.</p><p>3) Others: This category includes specialized pressure mapping devices used for specific applications, such as prosthetic fitting, ergonomic assessments, or industrial design. These devices are tailored to meet specific industry needs and may have unique features or measurement capabilities.</p></p>

<p><p>Surge Protective Devices (SPDs) are designed to protect electrical systems and devices from power surges or voltage spikes. AC Surge Protective Devices are used in alternating current (AC) electrical systems, such as those found in homes and commercial buildings. They safeguard against surges originating from the power grid. On the other hand, DC Surge Protective Devices are used in direct current (DC) electrical systems, commonly seen in telecommunications, solar power systems, and industrial applications. They provide protection against surges originating from sources like lightning strikes or switching operations.</p></p>

<p><p>The Dynamic Spinal Tethering System is a surgical technique used to treat scoliosis, a condition characterized by an abnormal curvature of the spine. It involves the use of screws, rods, and tensioning cords to correct and stabilize the spinal curvature. Unlike traditional spinal fusion surgery, which permanently fuses the vertebrae together, dynamic spinal tethering allows for continued growth and flexibility of the spine.</p><p>The market for dynamic spinal tethering systems is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of scoliosis and the growing demand for minimally invasive surgical procedures are the key factors driving market growth.

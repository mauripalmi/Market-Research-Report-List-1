<p><p>Personal car leasing refers to a long-term rental agreement where an individual can use a vehicle for a fixed period of time, usually between 2 to 4 years, by making monthly payments. Unlike purchasing a car, personal car leasing does not involve ownership of the vehicle and at the end of the lease agreement, the car is returned to the leasing company. This type of leasing offers flexibility and cost-effectiveness as it includes maintenance and other services within the monthly payment. </p><p>The personal car leasing market has witnessed significant growth in recent years. A key factor driving this growth is the changing consumer preferences towards more affordable and flexible mobility solutions. With increasing costs of owning a car, personal car leasing provides a viable alternative, allowing individuals to have access to a vehicle without the burden of maintenance, depreciation, and upfront costs. Additionally, the rise of ride-hailing services has also contributed to the growth of personal car leasing, as individuals who engage in these services prefer to lease a car rather than purchase one, given their fluctuating income.</p><p>Furthermore, the growth of the personal car leasing market can be attributed to the increasing awareness and adoption of electric vehicles (EVs). The demand for EVs is rising owing to their environmental benefits, and personal car leasing allows individuals to switch to EVs without the high upfront costs associated with purchasing such vehicles.</p><p>According to market research, the personal car leasing market is expected to grow at a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 8.4% during the forecast period. The trend of personal car leasing is expected to continue gaining traction in the coming years as more individuals seek flexible and affordable mobility solutions.</p></p>

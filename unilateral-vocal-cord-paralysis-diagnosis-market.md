<p><p>Unilateral vocal cord paralysis (UVCP) is a condition where one vocal cord is unable to move properly due to damage or dysfunction of the nerves controlling the vocal cords. It can result in voice hoarseness, difficulty speaking loudly, and difficulty swallowing. UVCP can occur due to various causes such as surgery, trauma, viral infection, tumors, or neurological disorders.</p><p>The market for unilateral vocal cord paralysis diagnosis is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years.

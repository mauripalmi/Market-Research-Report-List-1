<p><p>Hard carbon, also known as amorphous carbon, is a form of carbon that lacks a crystalline structure. It is predominantly derived from carbonaceous materials, such as coal, petroleum coke, or carbon black, through high-temperature processes. Hard carbon possesses unique properties, including high thermal and electrical conductivity, low coefficient of thermal expansion, high resistance to wear and corrosion, and excellent mechanical strength. These characteristics make it a versatile material with various applications across several industries.</p><p>The global hard carbon market is projected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The market is driven by factors such as increasing demand for lithium-ion batteries, which utilize hard carbon as an anode material. As the demand for electric vehicles and portable electronic devices continues to rise, the requirement for efficient and long-lasting batteries also grows, fueling the demand for hard carbon.</p><p>Additionally, the growing focus on renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar power, is expected to further contribute to the market's growth. Hard carbon is utilized in these applications to enhance the performance and efficiency of supercapacitors and fuel cells.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in technology, such as the development of new synthesis methods and the integration of nanotechnology, are propelling the market growth.

<p><p>The small satellite market is divided into two main types: microsatellites and nanosatellites. Microsatellites are small spacecraft weighing between 10 and 100 kilograms, and they are typically used for various applications such as remote sensing, communication, and weather monitoring. On the other hand, nanosatellites are even smaller, weighing less than 10 kilograms. They are often employed for technology demonstration, scientific research, and educational purposes. Both microsatellites and nanosatellites have gained popularity due to their lower costs, faster development times, and their ability to be launched in large constellations for enhanced coverage and capabilities.</p></p>

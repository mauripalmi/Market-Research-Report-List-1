<p><p>Mobile Mapping Systems (MMS) are used to collect geospatial data for mapping and surveying purposes. There are different types of MMS available in the market. The Direct Mobile Mapping System refers to a setup where sensors and data collection devices are mounted directly on a vehicle, such as a car or a van. This system allows for efficient and continuous data collection while the vehicle is in motion. On the other hand, the Backpack Mobile Mapping System involves wearable devices that are worn or carried by individuals, enabling data collection in areas inaccessible to vehicles, like narrow streets or pedestrian-only zones.</p></p>

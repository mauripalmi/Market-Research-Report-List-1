<p><p>Small hydropower refers to the utilization of water streams with a small potential energy gradient to generate electricity. It typically involves a turbine or generator installed in a localized water source, such as a small river or stream. Small hydropower projects are categorized as those with a capacity of less than 10 MW.</p><p>The small hydropower market is anticipated to witness growth at a CAGR of 3.90% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this growth, including the increasing demand for clean and renewable energy sources, government initiatives encouraging sustainable energy development, and the rising need for rural electrification.</p><p>Moreover, small hydropower is an attractive option as it has minimal environmental impact, does not require large reservoirs, and allows for distributed electricity generation. These advantages have led to the increased adoption of small hydropower projects across the globe.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements have boosted the efficiency of small hydropower systems, enhancing their commercial viability. The development of advanced turbine designs and control systems has increased the overall efficiency of small hydropower plants, leading to improved power generation capabilities.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is a growing focus on the development of micro hydropower projects, which have a capacity of less than 100 kW. These projects cater to the needs of remote locations and off-grid communities where access to electricity is limited. Additionally, the integration of digital technologies, such as remote monitoring and control systems, is gaining traction in the small hydropower sector, allowing for efficient operation and maintenance of generating units.</p><p>Overall, the small hydropower market is expected to witness steady growth in the coming years due to the increasing demand for renewable energy, government support, and advancements in technology.</p></p>

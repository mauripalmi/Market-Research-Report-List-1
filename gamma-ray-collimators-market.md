<p><p>Gamma ray collimators are devices used in imaging systems to limit the angle of radiation rays entering the detector. Panoramic collimators have a wider field of view and are used in applications where a broad image is needed. On the other hand, directional collimators have a more focused beam of radiation and are used in applications where precise localization of radiation sources is required. Both types play a crucial role in improving the accuracy and efficiency of gamma ray imaging systems in various industries.</p></p>

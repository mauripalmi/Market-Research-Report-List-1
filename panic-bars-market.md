<p><p>Panic bars, also known as crash bars or exit devices, are essential safety devices installed on emergency exits of buildings. These devices allow individuals to quickly and easily exit a building during an emergency, such as a fire or security breach. Panic bars are typically installed on doors that require a high volume of traffic, such as those in commercial buildings like offices, hospitals, schools, shopping malls, and hotels.</p><p>The panic bars market has witnessed considerable growth in recent years due to the increasing focus on building safety and security regulations. The market is primarily driven by the growth of the construction industry, particularly in developing countries, where a rise in commercial construction projects is occurring. Additionally, the increasing awareness of building safety among end-users and the implementation of stringent government regulations regarding building safety are also boosting market growth.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in panic bar technology, such as the incorporation of wireless and smart features, are also contributing to market expansion. These innovative devices allow remote monitoring and control, enabling real-time data analysis of panic bar usage, which enhances building security measures.</p><p>Recent trends in the panic bars market include the adoption of touchless panic bars and the integration of panic bar systems with building automation systems. Touchless panic bars utilize motion-sensor technology, eliminating the need for physical contact and preventing the spread of germs, making them particularly relevant in the current COVID-19 pandemic situation. The integration with building automation systems allows for centralized control and monitoring of panic bar operations, enhancing overall building safety and security.</p><p>In conclusion, the panic bars market is expected to witness significant growth with a projected CAGR of 13.6% during the forecast period.

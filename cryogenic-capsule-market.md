<p><p>The cryogenic capsule market is mainly classified into two types: Polypropylene and Polyethylene. Polypropylene cryogenic capsules are made from a thermoplastic polymer that provides good mechanical and thermal properties for preserving cryogenic samples. On the other hand, polyethylene cryogenic capsules are manufactured from a lightweight and impact-resistant material, offering excellent resistance to low temperatures. Both types of capsules are used for storing and transporting cryogenic materials such as biological samples and medical specimens, ensuring their preservation and safety in extreme cold conditions.</p></p>
